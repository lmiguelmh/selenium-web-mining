J.P. Morgan Has a New Twist on Blockchain
The bank’s project is being built off the publicly accessible Ethereum network code
By TELIS DEMOS
Updated Oct. 3, 2016 8:51 p.m. ET
J.P. Morgan Chase & Co. is working on a new twist on the technology behind bitcoin that would allow the bank to use a publicly available system for confidential transactions.
The move is a break with how some banks have approached the use of a bitcoin-style network, known generally as the blockchain, to try to replace creaking old systems with faster new technology.
